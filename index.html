Lucas Mayr

I am a cybersecurity architect at ON-RCPN and researcher at the Computer Security Lab of the Universidade Federal de Santa Catarina. I research classical and post-quantum digital signature schemes and manage the ON-RCPN PKI infrastructure.

Professional experience

I work as the cybersecurity architect at the Operador Nacional do Registro Civil de Pessoas Naturais do Brasil (ONRCPN), the agency responsible for the technological advancements of the Brazilian Civil Registry. Moreover, I research topics related to cryptography and public key infrastructure and work as a freelance in these subjects.

  • [Jan/2021–Today] Cybersecurity Architect (Fulltime)

    I am responsible for the design, implementation, and deployment of the ON-RCPN Public Key Infrastructure and its ceremonies and policies. I also lead the technical steps of mutual trust agreements between different PKI environments. I am also responsible for underlying CSIRT systems used by ON-RCPN.

  • [Mar/2021–Today] Cybersecurity architect (Freelance)

    • I am the liaison between LabSEC and the government agency responsible for the regulation and auditing of the communication and information technology sector in Mozambique, Instituto Nacional de Tecnologias de Informação e Comunicação, for all things PKI; which lead to the creation of the Mozambique PKI in 2023.

    • PKI specialist for the project titled "Serviços de Assinatura Digital de Documentos Eletrônicos para Instituições de Ensino" in conjunction with the Brazilian Network for Education and Research, which aims to increase the use of digital signatures in the academic environment of federal universities through novel advancements in PKI, digital certificates, and identity management.

  • [Aug/2020–Jun/2023] Project manager

    I led the project responsible for the Certificate Management System of the Brazilian Public Key Infrastructure (ICP-Brasil), which encompassed the development of a smartcard management system, the update of the underlying OpenSSL wrapper libcryptosec from version 1.0.2 to versions 1.1.x and 3.x, and the integration of Open Quantum Safe post-quantum bindings.

  • [Jul/2017–Aug/2020] Software developer

    I developed and maintained the Certificate Management System of the Brazilian Public Key Infrastructure (ICP-Brasil), its smartcards applets and OpenSC integrations; I also worked on the update process of these systems from Redhat 5 to Redhat 7. I am responsible for adding support for custom HSM engines and Edward curves to these applications. Lastly, I implemented symmetric key management systems, researched election protocols, and implemented a secure OTP service that runs inside HSMs.
